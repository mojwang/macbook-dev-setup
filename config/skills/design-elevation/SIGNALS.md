# Design Signals Index

Signals are recurring patterns that emerge from interrogation answers. After answering the 5 lenses, classify 2-4 signals that match. Use the recommended techniques for each signal to build a shortlist, then pick 2-3 from the intersection.

Signals are organized by the lens they most commonly emerge from, but any signal can surface from any lens.

## Audience Signals

**anxious-audience**
The viewer is uncertain, worried, or emotionally vulnerable. They need calm before they can act.
Techniques: Generous White Space, Muted Palette + Single Accent, Provider Photography, Warm Neutral Base, Conversational Headings, Urgency Without Anxiety

**skeptical-audience**
The viewer needs proof before they'll trust. They're evaluating credibility, not browsing.
Techniques: Credential Hierarchy, Testimonial Patterns, Social Proof Proximity, Contextual Benchmark, Anchoring

**overwhelmed-audience**
The viewer faces too many options or too much information. They need structure to make a decision.
Techniques: Progressive Disclosure Accordion, Progressive CTA Escalation, Tabbed Content, Progressive Data Disclosure, Card Grid with Hierarchy

**expert-audience**
The viewer is technically sophisticated. They want density, precision, and control — not hand-holding.
Techniques: Maximize Data-Ink Ratio, Direct Labeling, Small Multiples, Inverted Pyramid Layout, Highlight Table

**novice-audience**
The viewer is new to the domain. Concepts need grounding in familiar terms and clear sequences.
Techniques: Conversational Headings, Timeline Layout, Humanized Scale, Narrative Dashboard, Big Number Display

**proxy-researcher**
The viewer is researching on behalf of someone else — a caretaker, a buyer for their team, a family member.
Techniques: Credential Hierarchy, Pricing Table with Feature Matrix, Progressive Disclosure Accordion, Anchor Navigation

## Purpose Signals

**drive-conversion**
The page exists to make one thing happen — book, buy, sign up, call.
Techniques: Progressive CTA Escalation, Contrast Isolation, Sticky Booking Element, Directional Cues, Social Proof Proximity

**build-trust**
The page exists to establish credibility before any ask. Trust is the prerequisite to conversion.
Techniques: Generous White Space, Credential Hierarchy, Muted Palette + Single Accent, Testimonial Patterns, Provider Photography

**educate-explain**
The page exists to help the viewer understand a concept, process, or offering.
Techniques: Timeline Layout, Progressive Disclosure Accordion, Tabbed Content, Conversational Headings, Anchor Navigation

**differentiate**
The page exists to answer "why you and not them?" Stand out from alternatives.
Techniques: Asymmetric Layout, Editorial Photography, Branded Color Blocking, Distinctive Interaction Patterns, Typographic Personality

**retain-engage**
The page exists to keep existing users active and returning.
Techniques: Micro-animations on Interaction, Loading State Design, Progressive Data Disclosure, Card Grid Dashboard

## Context Signals

**first-impression**
First visit. The viewer is forming opinions in seconds. Every element either earns trust or loses it.
Techniques: Generous White Space, Contrast Isolation, Conversational Headings, Provider Photography

**high-stakes-decision**
The decision carries significant cost, health, legal, or life impact. The viewer needs reassurance, not pressure.
Techniques: Muted Palette + Single Accent, Credential Hierarchy, Testimonial Patterns, Urgency Without Anxiety, Warm Neutral Base

**comparison-shopping**
The viewer has multiple tabs open. They're looking for reasons to pick one option over another.
Techniques: Pricing Table with Feature Matrix, Anchoring, Social Proof Proximity, Contextual Benchmark, Card Grid with Hierarchy

**data-heavy-context**
The content is primarily quantitative. The viewer needs to extract meaning from numbers.
Techniques: Maximize Data-Ink Ratio, Direct Labeling, Gray + One Accent, Reference Lines and Bands, KPI Header Strip, The 3-Second Rule

## Constraint Signals

**information-overload**
More content exists than can be shown at once. The design must organize, not just display.
Techniques: Progressive Disclosure Accordion, Tabbed Content, Anchor Navigation, Inverted Pyramid Layout, Card Grid Dashboard

**low-attention**
The viewer has limited time or patience. The design must land its point immediately.
Techniques: Big Number Display, Contrast Isolation, The 3-Second Rule, Insight Title, Single-Stat Slide

**crowded-market**
Competitors look similar. Generic design = invisible design.
Techniques: Asymmetric Layout, Typographic Personality, Branded Color Blocking, Editorial Photography, Distinctive Interaction Patterns

**novel-concept**
The audience is unfamiliar with the category or model. They need the concept grounded before they can evaluate it.
Techniques: Timeline Layout, Conversational Headings, Humanized Scale, Progressive CTA Escalation, Anchoring

**premium-positioning**
The offering is priced above alternatives. The design must justify quality through craft and restraint.
Techniques: Generous White Space, Type Scale Consistency, Spacing Rhythm, Icon Coherence, Editorial Photography
